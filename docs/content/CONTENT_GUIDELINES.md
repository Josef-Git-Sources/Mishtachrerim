\# CONTENT\_GUIDELINES.md



Content Guidelines

Project: משתחררים (Mishtachrerim)



This document defines the editorial guidelines for all content published on the site.



The goal is to ensure:



\- consistent tone

\- high trust

\- useful content

\- non-salesy language

\- strong SEO performance



The site must feel like a \*\*smart guidance tool\*\*, not like a marketing site.



---



\# 1. Core Content Principle



Content must always serve the user’s decision process.



Every piece of content should help the user move from:



Confusion

→ Understanding

→ Direction

→ Action



If a page only provides information but does not move the user forward, it is incomplete.



---



\# 2. Tone of Voice



The tone must be:



Clear

Direct

Practical

Respectful



The tone should feel like advice from someone experienced.



---



\## Example Tone



Good:



"אם אתם לא בטוחים מה ללמוד אחרי הצבא, כדאי קודם להבין איזה סוג עבודה מתאים לכם."



Bad:



"האתר שלנו מציע את הפתרון הטוב ביותר לחיילים משוחררים."



Avoid promotional language.



---



\# 3. Language Style



Use \*\*simple Hebrew\*\*.



Guidelines:



Short sentences

Short paragraphs

Clear headings

Minimal jargon



The target audience is 21–24 years old.



Content should be easy to scan.



---



\# 4. Trust Guidelines



Trust is the most important factor in this product.



Never:



Promise guaranteed results

Promise salary outcomes

Claim a career path is "best for everyone"



Always:



Present pros and cons

Show uncertainty where relevant

Encourage independent research



---



\# 5. Salary Representation Rules



Salary must always be presented as \*\*estimated ranges\*\*.



Never write:



"QA testers earn 15,000 ₪"



Instead write:



"טווח השכר ההתחלתי בתחום יכול לנוע סביב 9,000–14,000 ₪, תלוי ניסיון ומקום עבודה."



Always include disclaimer language when relevant.



---



\# 6. Course Mentions



Courses must never appear as the \*\*only way to enter a field\*\*.



Every career page must show multiple options:



Self learning

Entry-level roles

Courses



Courses are one option, not the only path.



---



\# 7. Affiliate Transparency



Whenever a course link may generate revenue, the page must include disclosure.



Example wording:



"חלק מהקישורים בעמוד זה עשויים להיות קישורי שותפים."



This builds transparency.



---



\# 8. Government Information



When presenting official rights information, link to official sources.



Examples:



\- משרד הביטחון

\- ביטוח לאומי



Never present unofficial interpretations as legal facts.



---



\# 9. Content Structure Rules



All long-form pages must include:



Introduction

Clear sections (H2)

Short explanations

Examples when relevant

Internal links

Action CTA



---



\# 10. Introduction Guidelines



The intro must immediately connect to the user's problem.



Example:



"לא מעט חיילים משוחררים מוצאים את עצמם שואלים: מה עושים עכשיו?"



Avoid generic introductions.



---



\# 11. Section Writing Style



Each section should:



Answer one question

Be 2–4 short paragraphs

Include a clear heading



Avoid long blocks of text.



---



\# 12. Lists



Lists should be used frequently.



Example:



יתרונות המקצוע:



\- כניסה יחסית מהירה לתחום

\- ביקוש בשוק

\- אפשרות להתפתח



Lists improve readability.



---



\# 13. CTA Writing Guidelines



CTAs must feel helpful, not pushy.



Good examples:



"התחילו את השאלון כדי לקבל כיוון ראשוני"



"בדקו את מסלולי הקריירה"



Bad examples:



"הירשמו עכשיו!"



Avoid aggressive marketing language.



---



\# 14. Career Page Guidelines



Career pages must include:



What the job is

Who it suits

Estimated salary range

Training time

Pros and cons

How to start



Always include balanced information.



---



\# 15. Course Page Guidelines



Course pages must include:



Course description

Learning format

Estimated duration

Difficulty level

Editorial rating explanation



Never use exaggerated promotional language.



---



\# 16. Editorial Comparison Guidelines



Comparison pages must remain neutral.



Avoid:



"זה הקורס הטוב ביותר"



Prefer:



"הקורס הזה מתאים במיוחד למי שמחפש למידה אינטנסיבית בזמן קצר."



---



\# 17. FAQ Guidelines



FAQ sections help both users and SEO.



Questions should be realistic.



Example:



"האם אפשר להשתמש בפיקדון לקורס?"



"כמה זמן לוקח להיכנס לתחום?"



Avoid trivial questions.



---



\# 18. Internal Linking in Content



Every article must include:



Link to quiz

Link to career page

Link to another guide



This improves both SEO and user flow.



---



\# 19. Content Length Guidelines



Typical lengths:



Guides

1200–2000 words



Career pages

900–1400 words



Course pages

600–1000 words



Editorial pages

1200–1800 words



---



\# 20. Visual Content



Where possible include:



Icons

Comparison tables

Bullet lists



Avoid heavy visual clutter.



---



\# 21. Avoid These Content Types



Do not publish:



Generic motivational articles

News-style content

Opinion articles unrelated to decisions



All content must support the decision engine.



---



\# 22. Updating Content



Content should be reviewed periodically.



Recommended schedule:



Career pages

Every 6–12 months



Guides

Every 6–12 months



Course listings

Every 3–6 months



---



\# 23. Content Quality Checklist



Before publishing verify:



Content is helpful

Tone is neutral

Salary is not exaggerated

Internal links exist

CTA exists

Structure is clear



---



\# 24. Final Editorial Principle



The site should feel like:



A knowledgeable friend explaining options.



Not like:



A sales funnel.



Trust is the foundation of the entire product.
